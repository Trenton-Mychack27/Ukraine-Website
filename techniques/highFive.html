High Five Breathing

Target Age Group: Any age

Objective: ○ Gets students to focus on their breathing in the middle of a crisis. Works for anxiety, stress, and overstimulation.

Why This Works:


  • Paced Breathing:
    • Tracing up and down each finger naturally paces inhale/exhale (e.g., ~5 focused breaths across five fingers)

  • Crisis-Friendly:
    • No extra materials needed — ideal when supplies aren’t available

  • Mindfulness-Based:
    • Shifts attention to breath and sensation, reducing anxiety and panic

  • Relaxation:
    • Breath rhythm signals the brain to downshift, helping emotions settle

  • Symbolic:
    • Provides a go-to action when a panic attack is arising — something simple to hold onto

  • Universal & Simple:
    • Uses only your hand; can be done individually in any setting

Instructions:


  • Materials:
    • No materials required — just your hand and fingers

  • Hand Up:
    • Student (or adult) holds one hand up with fingers spread

  • Trace the Fingers:
    • Use the pointer finger of the other hand to slowly travel up and down each finger

  • Breathe with the Movement:
    • Breathe in while tracing up the finger
    • Breathe out while tracing down the finger
    • Repeat for all five fingers at a calm, steady pace
