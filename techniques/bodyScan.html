Body Scan Meditation

Target Age Group: 8-15 years old

Objective: To help overwhelmed children regain a sense of control over themselves and their situation.

Why This Works:


  • Mind-Body Connection:
    • Helps children recognize that their emotions and physical feelings are related

  • Mindfulness-Based:
    • Encourages deep breathing and emotional regulation

  • Escape:
    • Provides a physical break in the school day for children, when they may feel overwhelmed with the day

  • Universal & Simple:
    • Requires no materials and can be used in any setting

Instructions:


  • Sit Down and Close Eyes:
    • Sit in whatever configuration is comfortable for the students
    • Have everyone close their eyes

  • Regulate Breathing:
    • Guide students through 3–5 slow deep breaths to guide them into a calmer state
    • Instruct them to clear their minds of whatever they are thinking about

  • Start at Head:
    • Direct students to focus on the top of their head
    • Have them notice the feeling of their head on their shoulders
    • Focus on any feelings that may be related to the head, or any physical feelings occurring there
    • Any thoughts that come to their minds, just notice them and then let them float away
    • If they notice any tension or pain, release the feeling from the head

  • Work Their Way Down:
    • Have instructor list body parts and move down the body all the way to the toes
    • Allow students 15–30 seconds at each body part to observe any feelings from that body part
    • Reassure students that it’s ok if their minds wander, as long as they return to the activity when they realize

  • Return to Entire Body:
    • Once the toes have been reached, visualize the entire body together
    • Feel the body in its relaxed state
    • Slowly open eyes and return to normal

  • Optional:
    • Turn off lights and have children lay down if possible
    • Play calming music during the exercise
    • Tell students to visualize a laser going down their body, scanning each part, if they struggle to keep focus
