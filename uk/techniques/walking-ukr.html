Walking

Target Age Group: Anyone older than 5 years old

Objective: To help children take their mind off of what is causing them stress, and re-channel it into a simple physical activity.

Why it Works

Impvoves sleep:Being more physically active during the day helps children fall asleep easier at night because they exerted more energy during the day

Instructions:

  • Overview:
    • Walking is a physical exercise that can have many benefits for both the mind and body

  • Step 1: Try Taking 15–20 Minutes a Day
    • Walks can be as long or as short as one prefers, depending on endurance

  • Step 2: Inside vs. Outside
    • Whether you are inside or outside, walking provides benefits through movement
    • Walking outside offers extra benefits such as fresh air, new scenery, and natural inclines
    • If walking inside, try incorporating games to make it engaging
      • Example: put on songs you enjoy and walk for their duration, then add a “cool down” song to relax and collect your thoughts

  • Step 3: Set Goals During Your Walk
    • Challenge yourself to find or notice different things before finishing your walk
    • This acts as a great distraction from stressful thoughts
    • Examples of goals:
      • Spot someone wearing a pink shirt
      • Find three round rocks
      • Notice a cloud shaped like an animal
